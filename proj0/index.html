Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

Overview

In this mini-project, I explored the relationships between perspective, focal length (zoom), and the center of projection.

Part 1: Selfie — Wrong vs. Right

The close-up selfie accentuates the facial features closest to the camera, exaggerating my nose in comparison to the rest of my face. In contrast, the zoomed-in selfie taken further away is a more accurate representation of how I look, because the relative difference in distance between my various facial features are small.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Changing distance and zoom alters perceived depth. From farther away with more zoom, parallel lines appear more parallel and façades look flatter; moving closer with less zoom accentuates convergence and depth.

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

A shot of my friend demonstrating the dolly-zoom effect: the camera moves forward while zooming out to keep the subject the same size, dramatically changing the background perspective. The background appears to stretch or compress while my friend remains stable.
