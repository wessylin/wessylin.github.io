Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

CS180: Intro to Computer Vision & Computational Photography

Overview

In this mini‑project, I explored the relationships between perspective, focal length (zoom), and the center of projection. Through a selfie study, an architectural comparison, and a short dolly‑zoom clip, I observed how camera position and focal length shape perceived geometry and depth.

Part 1: Selfie — Wrong vs. Right

The close‑up selfie accentuates facial features nearest the camera, exaggerating my nose relative to the rest of my face. A zoomed‑in shot from farther away better represents how I look because the relative distance differences between features are smaller.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Changing distance and zoom alters perceived depth. From farther away with more zoom, parallel lines appear more parallel and façades look flatter; moving closer with less zoom accentuates convergence and depth.

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

A shot of my friend demonstrating the dolly‑zoom effect: the camera moves forward while zooming out to keep the subject the same size, dramatically changing the background perspective. The background appears to stretch or compress while the subject remains stable.
